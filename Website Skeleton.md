# Heshik Nandan - Personal Website Skeleton

## 1. Website Goal

Build a sharp, executive personal website for Heshik Nandan.

The website should position him as:

- A CIO and technology leader.
- An operator with experience analysing industries through Industry 4.0, digital maturity, sustainability, operational excellence, and industrial AI lenses.
- A strategic leader across product delivery, platform architecture, cloud, governance, and execution discipline.
- A practical decision-maker who works with numbers, trade-offs, and real operating constraints.

Primary purpose:

- Professional credibility.
- Personal brand.
- Clear introduction for senior executives, partners, collaborators, recruiters, and serious professional contacts.

Secondary purpose:

- A clean home for future thinking, strategy notes, and practical frameworks.

## 2. Positioning Guardrails

The website should not lean heavily into current-company-specific products, roadmap, pricing, architecture, or internal operating models.

Public positioning should say:

- Experienced in industrial transformation and Industry 4.0 maturity thinking.
- Comfortable translating complex operational frameworks into usable digital systems.
- Leads technology teams across product, engineering, QA, design, architecture, and governance.
- Works at the intersection of industry analysis, platform thinking, AI-enabled workflows, and execution.

Avoid saying publicly unless explicitly approved:

- Specific product names.
- Specific framework names if they are company-owned or commercially sensitive.
- "SuperApp" as a named roadmap.
- Internal pricing models.
- Company-server logic.
- Exact annual budget.
- Internal AI workflow details.
- Client, assessor, or partner commercial mechanics.

## 3. Target Audience

- CEOs, founders, board members, and senior executives.
- Industrial transformation partners and ecosystem builders.
- Technology leaders and product teams.
- Recruiters or organizations looking for CIO-level leadership.
- Professional contacts who want a quick understanding of Heshik's profile.

## 4. Design Direction

Style:

- Executive.
- Quiet.
- Sharp.
- Dense but readable.
- Modern technology-leadership feel.

Avoid:

- Overly flashy portfolio style.
- Startup founder hype.
- Generic personal branding language.
- Large motivational quotes.
- Cartoon visuals.
- Excessive gradients.
- Product-marketing language that sounds like current-company material.

Visual feel:

- Clean typography.
- Strong spacing.
- Mostly neutral palette with one confident accent color.
- Metrics should be visually prominent but not overexpose confidential context.
- Cards may be used for repeated items only, not for every section.

## 5. Tone of Voice

Tone:

- Direct.
- Professional.
- Strategic.
- Evidence-driven.
- No fluff.

Writing style:

- Short sections.
- Strong headings.
- Specific but not confidential.
- Tables where useful.
- Clear decisions and trade-offs.

Avoid:

- "Passionate about technology."
- "Innovative leader."
- "Results-driven professional."
- Generic corporate phrases unless backed by concrete evidence.

## 6. Recommended Site Structure

Version 1 should be a single-page website with anchored sections:

1. Home
2. Work
3. Industry Transformation
4. Technology Leadership
5. Thinking
6. About
7. Contact

Reason:

- Fast to build.
- Easier to polish.
- Lower content burden.
- Avoids overexplaining company products.
- Can become multi-page later.
